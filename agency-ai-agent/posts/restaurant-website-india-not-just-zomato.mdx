# Why Your Restaurant Needs a Website and Not Just Zomato

![Premium Restaurant Website Design in India](/images/featured-restaurant-website.webp)

You worked hard to build your restaurant. You perfected the menu, trained your staff, and made sure every plate going out looks and tastes great. But at the end of the month, when you check your numbers, Zomato and Swiggy have already taken their cut — 25 to 30% on every single order. That is not a small fee. That is a chunk of your profit, every day, every month, all year long.

Here is the truth that most restaurant owners in India are slowly realizing: depending only on food aggregators is like building your house on rented land. You have no control. The moment their commission goes up or your listing gets pushed down, your business suffers.

A restaurant website in India gives you something Zomato never will — a direct line between you and your customer, with no middleman taking a share of what you earn.

---

## Why Indian Restaurants Need a Website in 2026

### The Zomato and Swiggy Commission Problem Is Getting Worse

Let us talk numbers. If your average order value is ₹500 and Zomato takes 25%, you are giving away ₹125 per order. If you get 100 orders a month through them, that is ₹12,500 gone — every month. Scale that up and you are looking at lakhs of rupees leaving your business every year.

And it is not just the commission. These platforms control your reviews, your ranking, your visibility, and even which customers see your restaurant first. A competitor who pays more for ads on Zomato will show up above you. You have no say in that.

### Customers Are Searching Google Before They Decide Where to Eat

Think about what you do when you want to try a new place. You probably search something like "best biryani near me" or "cafes open late in Koramangala." That search happens on Google, not inside the Zomato app.

If your restaurant only exists on Zomato, you are invisible on Google. Customers who are already hungry and looking for a place just like yours will walk past you digitally without ever knowing you exist.

A restaurant website in India helps you show up when customers search on Google. That is free, consistent visibility that you own — not rented space on someone else's platform.

### Empty Tables Are a Sign Your Online Presence Is Not Working

Off-peak hours are painful. You have staff on duty, the kitchen is ready, and the tables are sitting empty at 3 PM on a Tuesday. This is not always a food problem or a location problem. Very often it is a visibility problem.

When someone searches for a lunch spot near your area on Google and your restaurant does not appear, that table stays empty. A good website with your menu, location, and photos gives Google enough information to show your restaurant to nearby customers — even during the hours when you need them most.

### You Do Not Own Your Presence on Zomato

Here is something worth sitting with. Every review you have collected on Zomato, every photo, every follower — none of that belongs to you. If Zomato changes its policies, increases commissions, or your account faces an issue, you could lose access overnight.

Your own website is yours. No one can take it away. Your domain, your content, your customer data — all of it stays with you.

## What Makes a Good Restaurant Website

### A Menu That Is Easy to Read on Mobile

According to a [recent IAMAI report](https://www.iamai.in/), more than 80% of restaurant searches in India happen on mobile phones. If your website menu is hard to scroll through or takes too long to load, people leave. A good restaurant website in India has a clean, fast-loading menu that works perfectly on any phone, without needing to pinch and zoom.

### A Way to Take Direct Orders or Table Bookings

One of the biggest advantages of having your own website is accepting orders directly. No commission. No middleman. The money goes straight to you.

Even if you are not ready for full online ordering, a simple table booking form makes a huge difference. Customers who find you on Google can reserve a table right there, without needing to call or message separately.

### Your Location, Timing, and Contact Front and Centre

Sounds basic, right? But you would be surprised how many restaurant websites bury this information. Your opening hours, address, Google Maps link, and phone number should be visible without scrolling. Customers who are ready to visit need this information in seconds.

### Real Photos of Your Food and Space

Stock photos fool nobody. Customers want to see what your actual food looks like and what the vibe of your restaurant is. Real photographs — even decent ones taken on a good phone — make your website feel trustworthy and appetizing.
