# Stop Paying 30% Commission: How to Get Direct Orders for Your Restaurant in India

![Premium Restaurant Website Mockup in Bangalore](/images/featured-restaurant-website.webp)

If you run a restaurant in Bangalore, you know the feeling. It’s 9 PM on a Saturday in Indiranagar. But at the end of the month, when you look at your settlement report, the smile disappears. 

You see that 25%, 28%, or even 30% commission taken off every single order. You worked for the ingredients, you paid the chef, you covered the rent, and you managed the stress—yet a tech platform took nearly a third of your revenue for simply "finding" the customer. 

**If your restaurant doesn't have its own website in 2026, you are paying a "lazy tax" to aggregators every single day.** You are losing money that belongs in your bank account, and you’re losing the chance to build a real relationship with your customers. In the competitive landscape of the restaurant industry in India, direct orders are no longer a luxury—they are a survival necessity.

## The Problem: The High Cost of Convenience in India

Let’s be honest: Zomato and Swiggy changed the game. They made ordering food easy for customers. But for restaurant owners in Koramangala or HSR Layout, that convenience has become a golden cage. 

### The Aggregator Trap: A One-Way Street
When a customer orders your famous butter chicken through an app, they aren't "your" customer. They are the app's customer. The app owns their email address, their phone number, and their ordering habits. According to a [2024 IAMAI report](https://www.iamai.in/), India now has over 820 million active internet users, with 90% of them accessing the web via mobile devices. This massive audience is being funneled into third-party apps, where you are just another listing among thousands.

You get a name on a receipt and a bag that leaves the door. If that customer wants food again tomorrow, they go back to the app—where your competitors are listed right next to you, often with "sponsored" tags to steal that customer back. You are essentially paying to have your own customers stolen from you.

### The Math Simply Doesn't Add Up
Margins in the food business in India are already thin. Between rising ingredient costs and labor, most restaurants aim for a 15-20% net profit. If an aggregator is taking 30%, they aren't just taking your profit—they are often making more money on the dish than you are. You are essentially doing all the hard work while they take the cream off the top. 

Think about it: for every ₹1,000 order, ₹300 goes to the platform. If you handle 50 such orders a day, that's ₹15,000 daily, or ₹4.5 Lakhs every month. Over a year, that's ₹54 Lakhs. That is the cost of not having a direct ordering strategy for your restaurant in India.

## 5 Practical Steps to Get Direct Orders for Your Restaurant in India

You don't need to be a tech genius to start this. Here are five actionable steps every Indian restaurant owner can take right now to reclaim their profits:

### 1. Claim and Optimize Your Google Business Profile
This is the most important "free" tool you have. Make sure your address, phone number, and hours are correct. Most importantly, add a "Website" link. When people search for you on Google Maps in Jayanagar or JP Nagar, they should be able to click through to your site immediately.
- **Pro Tip:** Post weekly updates and "Special Offers" directly on your Google profile with a link to your website's ordering page.

### 2. Get a Fast, Mobile-Friendly Website
With 90% of Indian users on mobile, if your website is a slow PDF menu or a broken page, they will leave. Your site needs to load in under 2 seconds and look beautiful on a smartphone.
- **Requirement:** Ensure your menu is "indexable" (text-based), not just an image. This helps you show up when someone searches for a specific dish like "best pepperoni pizza in Bangalore."

### 3. Add a Clear "Order Direct" Button
Don't hide your ordering link. Put a big, bright button at the top of your website that says "Order Direct & Save 10%." Give customers a reason to choose your site over an app. Even a small discount for the customer is still cheaper for you than a 30% commission.
- **Strategy:** Use a "sticky" header so the Order button is always visible, even as the user scrolls through your gallery.

### 4. Use QR Codes on Your Tables and Packaging
Every time a customer sits in your restaurant or receives a delivery bag, they should see a QR code. "Scan to see our menu and get 15% off your next direct order." This is how you "convert" app customers into direct customers.
- **Implementation:** Print stickers for your delivery bags. "Loved the food? Order directly next time and get a free dessert!"

### 5. Showcase Your Best Dishes with Real Photos
Stop using stock photos. Hire a local Bangalore food photographer or use a modern smartphone to take high-quality, "honest" photos of your best-sellers. People eat with their eyes first.
- **Optimization:** Use descriptive alt-text for your images (e.g., "Authentic Hyderabadi Biryani at our Koramangala Restaurant") to help your images show up in Google Image search.
